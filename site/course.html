The Course

Python for Finance & Algorithmic Trading

A cohort course built on the real thing: ASTI ("Skynet"), the automated mean-reversion screener/trader I built on Python and the Interactive Brokers (IBKR) API. You'll learn to build, backtest, and reason about systematic strategies — and, just as importantly, where they break. (They break. That's most of the curriculum.)

Who it's for

Finance-literate people who can read code (or will learn), and developers who want the finance side done right. Come in comfortable with the fact that most strategies fail — including good-looking ones, including some of yours. This is a course about disciplined process, not a lottery ticket with a syntax highlighter.

What we build, week by week

  1. Foundations & environment. Python for finance toolkit, data sources, clean project setup, and the mindset of systematic investing.
  2. Market data & the IBKR API. Pulling, cleaning, and storing data; connecting to a broker API safely (paper trading first).
  3. Signals & strategy logic. Mean reversion, momentum, and why the same idea can be both depending on who's selling it — building the logic behind ASTI.
  4. Backtesting honestly. Look-ahead bias, survivorship bias, transaction costs, slippage — every trick that turns a losing strategy into a gorgeous backtest, and how to stop lying to yourself with them.
  5. Risk & position sizing. Drawdown, volatility targeting, VaR/CVaR intuition, and sizing so one bad week is a bad week, not an obituary.
  6. Automation & going live (carefully). Scheduling, monitoring, failure modes, and the deeply unglamorous discipline that separates "running a system" from "feeding an ATM."
