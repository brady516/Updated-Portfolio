# Crypto: the protocol thesis vs. the casino

I'm not here to be a hater or a hype man — both are noise. There are two completely
different things wearing the same word "crypto": a serious **protocol thesis** (can
this technology do something valuable that nothing else can?) and a **casino** (a
24/7 global slot machine with a coin attached). Most of the trouble comes from people
buying the second while telling themselves it's the first.

## Signal vs. Noise — "it's going to the moon" *(free)*

> **The Noise:** price as the entire thesis. Number go up, therefore correct;
> influencers, tickers with dog names, "have fun staying poor."

A rising price is not a thesis — it's other people buying, which works right up until
it doesn't. "Utility" name-dropped with no answer to *what does this protocol actually
do that a database can't* is the tell that you're at the casino.

> **The Signal:** separate the technology question from the price question. Ask what
> the protocol *does*, who *needs* it, and whether the token is required for that — or
> just bolted on to sell.

## The Number — the float game *(free)*

Watch the **circulating vs. fully-diluted supply** and the unlock schedule. A token
"only up 3x" can be a disaster waiting if 80% of the supply is locked and about to
hit the market. Much of crypto's "performance" is a low-float illusion that dilutes
the people who showed up late. Read the tokenomics or be the exit liquidity.

## Wall Street Word Salad of the Week — Crypto Edition *(paid)*

**This week's salad:** *"A decentralized, community-governed protocol leveraging
blockchain to unlock a paradigm shift in value creation across the Web3 ecosystem."*

**Translation:** "We made a token."

**What it's hiding:** every noun is doing PR and none is doing work. "Decentralized"
and "community-governed" often mean "the founders hold the keys and the votes."
"Unlock a paradigm shift in value creation" is the most expensive way ever devised to
say nothing. Ask what it *does* on a Tuesday for a real user.

## The Read — telling the thesis from the casino *(paid)*

Three honest questions before any crypto position:

1. **What does the protocol actually do — and does it need a blockchain to do it?**
   Some genuinely do (censorship-resistant settlement, trustless coordination). Many
   are a normal app that added a token to raise money. If a database would work fine,
   the token is the product, and the product is you.
2. **Is the token *required* for the use, or *attached* to it?** Value accrues to
   tokens that the system genuinely needs to function — not to tokens slapped on for
   speculation. "How does this token capture the value it claims to create?" stops
   most pitches cold.
3. **What's the supply story?** Who holds it, what's locked, what unlocks when. The
   tech can be real and you can *still* get diluted to zero by insiders.

And the sizing rule that makes the whole category survivable: **this is venture-grade
risk, so size it venture-grade.** A small sleeve you can write to zero without changing
your life. If a position can't go to zero without wrecking you, it's not an investment,
it's a hostage situation. The casino is fine to visit with money you brought to lose —
just don't tell yourself it's the research account.

## What I'd Watch

Next crypto pitch you hear, strip every adjective and ask the one question the salad
exists to avoid: *what does it do, and why does it need a token?* If the answer is a
vibe, you're at the casino. Fine to play — just know which building you walked into.
