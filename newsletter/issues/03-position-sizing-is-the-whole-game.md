# Position sizing is the whole game

Everyone wants to argue about *what* to buy. Almost nobody wants to talk about
*how much* — which is unfortunate, because that's the part that actually decides
whether you survive being wrong. And you will be wrong. The question was never
accuracy; it was magnitude.

## Signal vs. Noise — "I'm going all-in on my highest-conviction idea" *(free)*

> **The Noise:** conviction as a position size. "I believe in this one, so it's 70%
> of the book."

Conviction measures how you *feel*. Position size should measure what happens if
you're *wrong*. Those are different planets. The most confident call I ever heard
was also someone's biggest loss — confidence is not a hedge.

> **The Signal:** size from the downside, not the daydream. Ask "if this goes to
> zero, am I still standing?" before "how rich does this make me?"

## The Number — the math of getting back to even *(free)*

Lose 50%, and you need a **100%** gain to break even. Lose 80%, you need **400%**.
Drawdowns aren't symmetric, and neither is the recovery — which is the entire
argument for not letting one position become your whole story.

## Wall Street Word Salad of the Week *(paid)*

**This week's salad:** *"We're running a concentrated, high-conviction book to
maximize our edge."*

**Translation:** "We made a few big bets and we'd like that to sound like a strategy."

**What it's hiding:** concentration cuts *both* ways — it maximizes the edge and the
faceplant equally. "High-conviction book" is the polished way to say "undiversified,"
which is fine if it's chosen on purpose and sized for the bear case, and reckless if
it's just enthusiasm with a Bloomberg terminal.

## The Read — sizing so one bad week isn't an obituary *(paid)*

A few rules that survive contact with a real market:

- **Risk a fixed fraction, not a feeling.** Decide in advance the max % of the
  portfolio any single idea can lose you. Size *back* into the position from there.
- **Volatility-adjust.** A 2% position in a quiet utility and a 2% position in a
  pre-revenue biotech are not the same bet. Size down what swings harder.
- **Correlation counts as concentration.** Five "different" positions that all rise
  and fall together are one position wearing five name tags (more on that in a
  later issue).
- **Pre-commit the exit.** Decide what breaks the thesis *before* you're emotionally
  married to it. The position size you can hold calmly through a drawdown is the
  only one that's really yours.

The boring truth: you can be right less than half the time and still do well if your
winners are sized bigger than your losers. You can also be right most of the time and
blow up, if the one time you're wrong you'd bet the house.

## What I'd Watch

Find your largest position. Ask what happens to your year if it drops 50% next
month. If the answer makes your stomach drop, the position isn't too risky — it's
too *big*. That's a sizing problem, and sizing problems are fixable.

*(A way of thinking about risk, not individualized investment advice.)*
