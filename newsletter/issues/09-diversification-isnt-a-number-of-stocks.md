# Diversification isn't a number of stocks

"I own 30 stocks, I'm diversified." Maybe. If all 30 are mega-cap tech, you don't
own 30 stocks — you own one bet with 30 ticker symbols and a comforting sense of
breadth. Diversification isn't a *count*; it's about owning things that don't all
fall down at the same time.

## Signal vs. Noise — "I own 30 stocks, I'm diversified" *(free)*

> **The Noise:** diversification = a big number of holdings.

You can hold 50 names and have one risk. You can hold 8 and have several. What
matters is **correlation** — how your holdings move *together* — not the length of
the list.

> **The Signal:** real diversification is uncorrelated exposures, not a long
> watchlist. Count the bets, not the tickers.

## The Number — correlation, not count *(free)*

Two holdings with a correlation near **1.0** are effectively one position for
risk purposes. The diversification benefit comes from owning things with *low or
negative* correlation — the stuff that zigs when the rest zags. Past correlations
also have a nasty habit of all running to 1.0 in a crisis, exactly when you needed
them not to.

## Wall Street Word Salad of the Week *(paid)*

**This week's salad:** *"A diversified, multi-asset solution providing balanced
exposure across a broad opportunity set."*

**Translation:** "We own a lot of different things."

**What it's hiding:** "broad opportunity set" says nothing about whether those
things actually behave differently. A fund can be diversified by *label* and
concentrated by *risk* — six sleeves that all ride the same factor. Look through the
labels to the exposures.

## The Read — false diversification, real diversification *(paid)*

- **Look through to factors.** Sector, size, geography, and style (growth vs. value,
  rate-sensitive vs. not). Thirty growth tech names = one factor wearing thirty hats.
- **Stress-test together, not apart.** Ask how the *whole book* behaves in one bad
  scenario — a rate spike, a recession, a tech derating — not how each name looks
  alone.
- **Mind the hidden single bet.** Your job, your house, your region, and your
  portfolio can all be the same economic bet. Diversify the life, not just the
  brokerage account.
- **Diversification has a cost, and that's the point.** If everything you own is up
  together, you're not diversified — you're concentrated and lucky. Real
  diversification means something you own is *always* disappointing you. That's it
  working.

## What I'd Watch

Group your holdings by what actually drives them — rates, growth, the economy, one
sector. If most of your portfolio lands in one bucket, you have one bet, not a
portfolio. Better to know that on a calm Tuesday than to discover it in a crash.
